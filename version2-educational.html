Why So Many Adults Wake Up Tired, Even After 7 Hours in Bed

Poor sleep is not always about how long you sleep. Sometimes it is about how deeply your body is able to rest.

Sleep Quality Matters More Than Just Sleep Time

Many people think they simply need more hours in bed. But if you are waking up during the night, tossing and turning, or starting the day tired, your sleep quality may not be where it needs to be.

Key Insight: Research suggests that sleep quality - how well you cycle through sleep stages - may be just as important as total sleep duration for feeling rested.

Signs Your Sleep Quality May Need Support:

  • Waking up multiple times per night
  • Difficulty falling back asleep after waking
  • Feeling tired despite getting 7+ hours
  • Racing thoughts at bedtime
  • Needing caffeine to function in the morning

A Better Bedtime Routine Starts Before Your Head Hits the Pillow

A strong nighttime routine may include:

  • Turning off screens earlier (blue light affects melatonin)
  • Reducing late caffeine (stays in system 6-8 hours)
  • Keeping a consistent bedtime (supports circadian rhythm)
  • Creating a calm environment (cool, dark, quiet)
  • Supporting relaxation before bed
